About Me

Hi! I’m Minh and I’m currently studying at Cornell University as an undergraduate. I’m majoring in Computer Science and minoring in Electrical and Computer Engineering. I finish my undergraduate degree in Fall 2024, but I also plan to complete a Masters of Engineering in Computer Science by Spring 2024. My main interests in CS are software engineering and computer vision. Outside of academics, I enjoy dancing and other forms of art!

Early Life

I grew up in Maryland and have been there for most of my life. I went to Elanor Roosevelt high school which had a Science and Technology program that allowed me to develop my interests and skills in engineering.

At Roosevelt, I took many hands on introductory engineering classes. Some of the projects I worked on was building a building out of balsa wood, creating a pin hole camera, and making a website.

I also took my first Computer Science class in high school, but it didn't foster much of an interest due to the poor teaching style of the teacher.

HS Internship

One of the main draws of the Science and Technology program at Roosevelt was the senior year research project. For the research project, Roosevelt helped match us with internships in the area. I matched with the Johns Hopkins Applied Physics Lab and worked with Dr. Edward Gaddy. My project was to do data analysis on the Parker Solar Probe.

This project was the one that sparked my interest in Computer Science. A lot of the work was computational and graphical, but this was the first time I experienced the workflow of coding. I had to learn to import data, clean it up, and modify it with different mathematical formulas.

The research project component of the work taught me how to read and interpret research papers. It is one of the main reasons that I'm interested in research now in College.

Cornell University

I'm currently a Senior at Cornell University majoring in Computer Science. Cornell has been a very important part of developing my career and interest in CS. A lot of the classes have taught me how to be a cleaner coder, but most of my experience here has come from project teams and research.

I worked with Cornell Mars Rover on their electrical subteam for a year where I built a servo control board from scratch and developed the code frame to interface and control the servos. This experience really helped bridge hardware and software for me and gave me an appreciation for both.

I've spent a year researching in the Computer Vision lab where I worked more in data analysis and pipelining. This has definitely helped develop my coding style.

I plan to spend another year completing an MEng in CS where I hope to delve further into robotics and computer vision.

Industry Experience

My first internship was at Capital One where I worked on a front-end team. This experience was definitely different from my previous work. It was a glimpse into the real world and how teams work together on a unified code base. The experience of working on a team at Capital One has definitely shaped my perception of how working will be.

Apart from the experience working on a team, this was also my first experience working in front-end and it has shown me that I do enjoy software development as well. This gives me more flexibility in the jobs that I can do post-grad.
